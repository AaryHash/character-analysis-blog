Related

We here at Creating Life know that many creative people, people who want to develop media of their own, will be searching for information such as that found on this blog. As such, we wanted to give them access to related materials that they could use to complete their creative projects.

In a more and more AI-riddled world, with more slop being thrown onto the Internet for mass consumption, we hope that providing greater creative access to readers will remind people of what we can do as a society when we treat art with the respect it deserves.

Making Video Games

Interested in making your own video game? Go to Develop Games! This site catalogues a variety of different services you can use to create different parts of your game. It easily lists options for art programs, coding platforms, and music-making software, as well as describes when they are best used.

Character Creation

Interested in designing characters for your world? Go to Hero Forge! This site has a variety of different creatures that you can design and make your own, adding clothing and armor, tools, decoration, and more. By creating a visual representation of the idea that was already in your mind, you can take a first step towards developing your creative project.

Art Fundamentals

Interested in art and animation but don't know where to start? Go to this Reddit Forum! The owner of the post created a list of things for someone who wants to learn, following the path from knowing nothing about art to being able to both draw and create 2D frame-by-frame animations on their own. The best part is that the resources you need are free/cheap and widely available.

Worldbuilding

Interested in building the world for your story? Go to World Anvil! This site gives you access to clean, easy to follow diagrams that allow you to build your world. You can literally create the world with maps, or you can create the societal layout of the world with all of its characters and societies and how they interact.

Inspiration

Need some ideas for how to create a film project from home? Go to the Rooster Teeth Archive! This company has been around for 21 years and it was originally started by 4 men who loved film, working out of a garage. The site has a catalog that is easy to search, documenting all of the content the company has ever produced. By sifting through the wide variety of media they have made, you can get inspired and think of what you can also do from your own home.
